
# Analogies working with AI

> for presentation

as the project grew

- I had to develop the rules for the AI to follow
- at first one set of rules was enough
- but then the AI, the simple models that I have to use for building can only hold so much information in their context window (their efficient working memory)
- so I had to develop a system of rules that would be specific to the different categories of tasks (sections)
- I had to build rules of communication between the different parts of the system (sections), how they should communicate ( intereact with other sections)
- I had to provide only the information they needed
- I had to implement standards and guidelines
- But I had also to implement those rules wisely, so that the AI could understand them and follow them, while still making it possible for the AI to perform the different tasks required, while ensuring that the system was at optimal efficiency, that it had the information and tools it needed, and while still making it possible for me to understand and use the system.

- I had to have a vision and a complete implementation plan, and detailed tasks
- I had to define exactly what I wanted and how I wanted it done

- I discovered, at first project management, but after a while, while the complexity of the system grew, I learned how to manage a workforce, I became a CEO ...
- I also learned that administration, rules and management was not totally unecessary, but if done correctly it could make the system more efficient, resilient and easier to use because it then could keep my development system towards the ultimate goal and allowed harmonization between the different parts of the system.

- I had to stop development, because the system could not be efficient while the rules and guidelines were not clear enough, and while the AI could not understand them well enough to follow them correctly. Developping while the rules were not clear enough was like building a house on a shaky foundation, it created more problems and more work, that then needed to be fixed, realined. So it created extra work. Consequently I learned that no employes could do good work if the rules, guidelines, standards, objectives, and so on were not adapteed and clear enough.

- It highlighted the importance of dependencies of the adminstration / supporting part of the work, on the development part of the work, and vice versa.

- Thus I decided to give some free days to my little AI workforce, while I was fixing the rules ... and guidelines, to avoid unnecessary exhaustion of my credits. I exhausted however very fast my credits to make complete tasks and orchestration, but then the rules were developped and clear, so I could resume the work, while the heavy (important models) were relaxing. They have a lot more of relaxing generally from the workforce.... maybe because there are fewer of them.  

- the heplfull worker : agents and models are not fixed. They have a certain amount of memory and working capacity, and a certain quota (price). For the low price models, you can use it more. And you basically can do many many requests, but it needs more detailed and specific information to work. Then depending on when this model has been trained, re-trained or realigned (equivalent to a fresh night sleep or reset) it might behave differently, and be more optimal (or less that the same model you used yesterday). You have many different you can use for a same model, so this is equivalent to different employees doing different tasks. When you send a chat, it goes to a node - that have the model implemented, and depending on its state of freshness or instructions it can behave differently than another chat. So when you are in a chat that behaves better than usuall, and is extremly helpfull, you try to stay in this chat, using this model as much as possible ... untill it collapse of exhaustion, because you really squize out of it the most work possible. The problem is that this node and good model reset is not used only by you ... so you are not the only one that finds out that this one was particularly helpfull, so many many people try to squeeze the most of this model and nodes ... untill well it cant do anything anymore - it is just overwhelmed by the the amount of work to do - then well it starts just to stop working much faster that it used to - it takes a lot of breaks and you need to restart / or remind it to continue ... and its work capacity is really decrease (sometime its like it goes on sick leave, because you cannot do anything with it) - its just too many people wanting things from it at the same time and it becomes useless.

So you can try a more expensive worker, but then you are also limiting and reducing your global workforce capacity because you have so much amount of ressources (tokens) to spend. And that does not necessary work either - because yes, you are not the only one that want to have its stuff done ASAP.... so sometimes, you just need to wait that the worked is freshened up and has recovered working capacity. ... and yes, things do not go as fast as you expected. The point, is that there is always a limit of what you can do, even with AI. You need to prioritize what you ask it to do, and you can do other tasks when you have to wait for its recovery time.

You really need to go into ressource management and appropriate use. For example the planning workers helps you to define priorities, keep in line with the goal and create detailed tasks that can be used by the cheap, many little workers. If you do not use them regularly, at one point you get stuck because the little workers do not have tasks and plans defined and they must stay idle or try to define what they have to do themselves - which well, they might not have the global view and then have different detail oriented prioritoes. And because the amount of usage of planning workers is limited per week, at a different rate than your little workers, then you need to use them parsimoniously when needed but also not avoid to use them because you need to plan ahead to ensure that saving their effort does not lead to idle time for little workers. Its all a question of proper ressources usage and appropriate management.
